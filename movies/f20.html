Story

An unnamed narrator struggles with insomnia and a growing sense of emptiness in his white-collar life. He forms an underground fight club with the charismatic and mysterious Tyler Durden, leading to an anarchic journey of self-discovery and rebellion against societal norms.

As the fight club evolves into a dangerous organization, the narrator’s identity and perception of reality are challenged. Themes of consumerism, mental health, and personal freedom are explored through a mix of dark humor, violence, and psychological tension.

Fight Club is renowned for its provocative narrative, visual style, and the performances of Edward Norton, Brad Pitt, and Helena Bonham Carter. It has become a cult classic with lasting impact on pop culture.
