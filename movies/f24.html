Story

This anthology film follows three interconnected stories about familial relationships across different parts of the world. In the “Father” segment, an adult daughter and son visit their distant father; “Mother” focuses on sisters and their author mother in Dublin; and “Sister Brother” explores the bond between siblings in Paris as they navigate shared history and personal questions of identity.

Written and directed by Jim Jarmusch, the film blends humor and quiet drama to depict the subtleties of family dynamics, memory, and connection. It premiered at the 2025 Venice International Film Festival and won the Golden Lion, the festival’s highest award.

The narrative doesn’t hinge on action but on character moments and emotional rhythms, making it an intimate and reflective experience about the ties—sometimes awkward, often unresolved—that bind parents and adult children.
