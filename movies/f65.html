Story

12-year-old Musa lives in a rural Anatolian town with his father, Beko, who raises rabbits. Beko learns that families with disabled children can receive government support, and he asks Musa to pretend to be disabled. This decision changes Musa’s life, and attending a special school introduces him to freedom, friendship, and imagination.

At school, Musa forms a close bond with a girl named Nergis and begins a quiet but determined resistance to protect his rabbits and his innocent world. The story explores childhood, friendship, and the pursuit of freedom in a melancholic yet hopeful tone.

The film brings together the harsh rules of the adult world and the fragile imagination of childhood, offering the audience an emotional and heartfelt journey.
